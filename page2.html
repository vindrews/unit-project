Half-Life's Sequels

Half-Life is a series with 15 games in it, and yet there are only two that follow proper linear progression. Half-Life 1 and Half-Life 2 are about as linear as one can get with Valve games. After the release of Half-Life 2, Valve released Half-Life 2: Episode 1, which was a continuation of the story of Half-Life two. They then released a bunch of other games with titles like this, some of them following the progression of the story and some of them just being little side quests. A running joke in the Valve fandom is that the company can never make a third game. Half-Life, Portal, and Team Fortress seem to be stuck with only a sequel for the forseeable future.

Portal Co-Op

When originally creating Portal 2 Co-Op, the developers were going to have a very long and gruesome death animation each time a player died. More specifically, it was of the robot they played as being slowly crushed by a garbage compactor. As playtesting went on, they realized that it was making players be way more cautious, take less risks, and overall have less room to be a bit stupid because they didn't want to sit through the animation every time they died. The developers didn't want this, the point of the game was to try out solutions to puzzles even if theyre stupid! Half the fun is messing up and getting to try again. So, the devs chose to make the death animation much quicker and sillier. Now, the robots spark and flail around a bit when they get dropped in water, then immediately respawn. This immediately fixed the problem. Players were instantly more likely to try out dangerous solutions to their problems because they were no longer being punished with long animations when they did. Now, dying in Co-Op is a minor inconvenience at worst, and an absolutely hilarious thing to do to your friends at best.
